### Part 3: Visualization
##### For this part, I selected the variables Flipper Length, Culmen Depth, and Culmen Length to create a 3D plot against their mass. The reason is that I believe the larger these body parameters, the more likely that an indivudual will have a higher mass. As a result, I plot the 3D graph as follows:
```python
# Creating a 3D plot
fig = px.scatter_3d(penguins,
                    x = "Flipper Length (mm)",
                    y = "Culmen Length (mm)",
                    z = "Culmen Depth (mm)",
                    color = "Body Mass (g)",
                    opacity = 1,
                   title = "Body Mass vs. Body Parameteres")

fig.update_layout(title_x=0.035,title_y=0.965,
                  margin={"r":0,"t":0,"l":0,"b":0})
fig.show()
plotly.io.write_html(fig, "Penguins.html")
"""
In our 3D graph, the ligher color, the heavier the body mass. As we could see, 
the more to the positive side of each axis, the lighter the color, or in other words, 
the heavier the body mass. It confirms our hypothesis.
"""
```
